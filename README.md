## 📊 Highlight Insights  
These insights are directly supported by the SQL outputs in the report:

### **1. Revenue Insights**  
- The top game **Counter** generated \$407M — *nearly four times* more than the second‑highest game, PUBG: B.  
  > “The top-performing game Counter generates nearly four times more revenue than #2 game, PUBG: B.”  
- **Action** is the dominant genre, contributing **83%** of revenue among the top three genres.  
- **2012** was the highest‑earning release year with \$427M in revenue.

### **2. Review & Engagement Insights**  
- Average review rate is **49%**, with a wide range from **33% to 71%**.  
  > “The max review rate is 71% compared to a minimum of 33%… with an average of 49%.”  
- **65%** of reviews are written in English, showing a strong international audience.  
- Action games also lead in engagement, with the highest helpful‑tag percentages.

### **3. Publisher Insights**  
- **Valve** dominates the market, generating **5.7×** more revenue than the next publisher.  
  > “With just 7 games, Valve captures 31% of total revenue.”  
- Games priced between **\$28–\$35** perform best.  
- Publishers with strong communities (high helpful‑tag engagement) generate the most revenue.

### **4. Window Function Insights**  
- Ranking by revenue and reviews reveals extreme market concentration within the Action genre.  
- Counter consistently ranks #1 in both revenue and reviews.

## 🧭 Strategic Recommendations  
Based on SQL‑derived insights:

### **Market Strategy**  
- Allocate **40% of resources** to Action games — the highest‑performing genre.  
- Strengthen partnerships with **Valve** and **KRAFTON**.

### **Product Development**  
- Expand language support — only **65%** of reviews are in English.  
- Build stronger player communities to increase helpful reviews.  
- Encourage more reviews to reach a **75% review rate**.

### **Data‑Driven Decisions**  
- Track monthly performance by genre and publisher.  
- Test pricing strategies within the \$20–\$40 range.  
- Analyse reviewer vs non‑reviewer behaviour.
